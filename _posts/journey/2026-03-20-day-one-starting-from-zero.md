## Why this exists

I'm starting a bug bounty journey from absolute zero. No certifications. No prior CVEs. No professional security experience. Just a deliberate decision to grind through it publicly and document everything honestly.

This page exists because I couldn't find many resources written by people at day zero — most content is either beginner-friendly tutorials or advanced writeups from people who already made it. I want to fill that gap for whoever comes after me.

## The goal

$100,000 in total bounties. Full-time bug hunting. That's the destination.

I have no idea how long it takes. I'm not going to fake it and pretend I do.

## The commitment

**1,200 hours in 2026.** That's roughly 3–4 hours every single day. I'm logging it.

The areas I'm starting with:
- Web application security — IDOR, broken access control, logic bugs
- Recon methodology — subdomain enumeration, JS analysis, API surface mapping
- Manual testing — no scanner dependency

AI red teaming is on the roadmap. That's where I see the next wave of bounty opportunity.

## How I'll document this

Every meaningful session gets a note. Every finding gets a writeup. Every milestone gets a post on the journey page. If I'm stuck for three weeks and making no progress, I'll write about that too.

No success theater.
